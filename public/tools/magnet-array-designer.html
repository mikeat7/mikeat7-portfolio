1 · What Is a Magnet Array?

A magnet array arranges individual magnets in a precise geometric pattern so their combined fields interact — reinforcing in some places and canceling in others — creating a larger, more complex field than any single magnet could produce alone.

2 · The Flower of Life — 12 Magnets

Exactly 6 equal circles fit perfectly around 1 equal circle — geometric necessity, not approximation. The 12-magnet layout uses: 1 center + 6 inner ring + 5 outer ring (the 6th outer position is intentionally omitted).

3 · The Missing 13th Magnet

The Flower of Life has 13 natural node positions. The 12-magnet design deliberately omits one outer North position. The adjacent South magnet has no opposing North to complete its horizontal loop — so the field curves vertically, perpendicular to the disc. This drives the field upward and downward. When cells tile in a fractal, the 13th gaps at cell boundaries align and constructively interfere — this is why the fractal is more than additive.

6 · Magnet Shape and Thickness

  • Disc: wide pole face → broad field, best lateral coupling between neighbours. Recommended.
  • Cylinder: narrow pole face → focused deep field along axis. Less coupling.
  • Block: corner field distortion — less uniform, harder to pack.

Optimal diameter-to-thickness ratio: 3:1 to 4:1. Thicker magnets reach further before field drops below threshold. Stacking thinner discs achieves the same effect — 4 × 2mm = 8mm gives 3.5:1 on a 29mm magnet.

6 · Housing and Assembly

  • Good materials: hardwood, MDF, acrylic, 3D-printed PLA/PETG, epoxy resin, bamboo
  • Bad materials: steel, iron, ferromagnetic stainless — absorb and redirect field
  • No metal fasteners near the array — use wooden pegs, bamboo dowels, or double-sided tape
  • Assembly order: center first, then alternate inner ring, then remaining inner, then alternate outer, then remaining outer. Epoxy before moving.

PHYSICS BASIS
The 13th gap removes one cancelling magnet from the outer ring. By Maxwell superposition, the two flanking magnets on either side of the gap are no longer opposed — their combined field projects more strongly along the gap axis than it would in a complete ring. Pointing both outer cell gaps inward directs this stronger emission toward the array center, improving coverage of the tray's hardest-to-reach zone. The center cell's gap points downward so its associated null point forms below the array rather than in the growing medium.

Note: this model uses dipole field approximation (accurate for distances > 2× magnet diameter). The recommendation is physically well-motivated but should be verified with a calibrated Hall-effect gaussmeter before drawing scientific conclusions.

Application Guides

🌱 Microgreens — Mold Suppression

Placement: Directly under the tray — root zone within 10–15mm of device face. Config: Single cell or 3-cell row, 29mm N52 ×4 stack. At 10mm: 4,000–7,000 Gauss. Targets Botrytis, Pythium, Fusarium (all iron-dependent). Experiment: Two identical trays, one over device, one not. Photograph daily, weigh at harvest.

🐝 Beehives

Placement: On bottom board centered under brood nest. Orient 13th gap toward entrance. Config: 7-cell fractal, 29mm N52 ×4. At 80mm: ~400–600 Gauss — within mite treatment patent range (1,600–2,800 G/cm³ at mite location requires closer placement or stronger array). Board size: 7-cell array ≈ 370mm — matches Langstroth internal width.

🐱 Cats and Small Animals

Placement: Under pet bed or mat, animal 15–30cm above. Config: Single cell, 20–29mm N42/N52. At 20cm: 30–80 Gauss — within veterinary PEMF therapeutic range for soft tissue healing and arthritis. Avoid near animals with implanted devices.

👤 Human Passive Exposure

Placement: Under chair cushion, mattress topper, or meditation mat. Body 20–40cm above. Config: 7-cell fractal for bed coverage. At 30cm: 20–50 Gauss. PEMF is FDA-cleared for bone fracture healing; static magnet therapy shows positive signals for pain relief and sleep quality. Contraindicated with pacemakers, implanted defibrillators, cochlear implants.

🛏 Bed-Scale Array (Depth 3)

A 19-cell depth-3 array with 29mm magnets spans ~105cm — queen mattress width. Build as 19 individual cells in a large carrier board (two-piece construction for boards exceeding 24 inches). Field at 30cm: ~80–200 Gauss across the full sleeping surface. Total magnets: 228 × 4 stacked = 912 individual discs.
