What it measures

CrystalScope scores six text-level signals that tend to co-appear with rigorous reasoning: argument connectives, epistemic hedging, conceptual density per sentence, cohesion markers, cross-domain vocabulary, and quantitative precision. These are proxies — surface features that well-reasoned text often exhibits. They are not the reasoning itself.

What it cannot reach: the true CRYSTAL signals

The CDM (Crystal Depth Metric) framework this tool is based on defines four signals that exist inside a transformer model's computation. No browser tool can access them:

Entropy Collapse (ΔH ≥ 2.3 bits)
When a model reasons toward a conclusion, the probability distribution over its next possible tokens narrows sharply. Entropy (H) measures that uncertainty — high entropy means many continuations seem equally plausible; low entropy means the model has committed to a direction. A collapse of 2.3 bits or more signals genuine convergence, not fluent pattern-completion. This lives in the model's probability layer, invisible in any text it produces.
Convergence Ratio (≤ 0.12)
At each layer of a transformer, an internal vector called the "hidden state" encodes the model's evolving representation of meaning. During deep reasoning, these vectors stabilize across layers — they stop drifting and settle into a coherent position. A ratio below 0.12 means that stabilization has occurred: like a tuning fork reaching its resonant frequency, the model's representation has found its answer. The output text cannot tell you this happened.
Attention-Gini Delta (≥ 0.28)
Transformer models distribute "attention" across all tokens in context simultaneously. During genuine reasoning, this attention sharpens — it concentrates on the tokens that actually matter rather than spreading uniformly. The Gini coefficient (borrowed from economics, where it measures income inequality across a population) quantifies that concentration. A delta of 0.28 or more means attention has focused significantly since the start of generation. You can see what the model wrote; you cannot see where it was looking.
Basin-Escape Probability (≥ 0.88)
In the high-dimensional space of possible outputs, a model can become trapped in a "basin" — a region of similar, fluent-sounding responses that are essentially sophisticated pattern-completions rather than inferences. High basin-escape probability means the reasoning state is robust: perturbing the inputs slightly does not change the conclusion. This distinguishes genuine inference from confident confabulation. Measuring it requires systematically probing the model with variations — something no single text can reveal.
What recent test cases showed

A children's story (narrative fiction) scored low on argument architecture and domain reach — not because it was poorly written, but because narrative writing isn't trying to construct an argument. It's trying to create a world, a character, an emotion. CrystalScope now detects narrative genre and stands down rather than giving absurd advice like "add inference markers."

A philosophical encyclopedia entry (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy) scored in the mid-range — not because the philosophy was shallow, but because analytic philosophy argues by presenting and contrasting positions rather than by explicit connective chains. "Nagel holds that subjective experience cannot be reduced to physical description" is a different argumentative syntax than "therefore, it follows that consciousness is non-physical." The tool partially misses the former style.

A speculative science essay on the Primary Organism and Dark Matter scored high on domain reach (100 — genuinely rare) and conceptual density (82 — real and earned), while scoring lower on argument architecture because its primary mode was analogy and sustained assertion rather than connective chains. The tool accurately described the structure. It said nothing about whether the ideas were right, interesting, or original — because it cannot.

The shadow

A careful thinker, writing with precision, leaves structural traces in their text — explicit connectives, calibrated hedges, cross-domain references, numbered evidence, precise distinctions. CrystalScope detects those traces. It is reading the shadow cast by deep reasoning on the surface of the text.

A shadow has a real relationship to what casts it. But it flattens the object, loses dimension, and changes with the angle of the light. A manifesto casts a different shadow than a scientific paper. A poem casts almost none — and may be the most profound thing in the room.

Scores above 80 are genuinely rare. Above 90 is near-theoretical — it would require a text that simultaneously maxes most signals: explicitly chained inferences, calibrated hedging, dense cross-domain concepts, tight structural cohesion, and quantitative precision. Such texts exist. They are uncommon.
